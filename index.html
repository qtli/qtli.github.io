Biography

Hi! I’m a third-year PhD student in computer science at The University of Hong Kong, advised by Lingpeng Kong and BCM Kao. I have previously interned at Tencent AI Lab, working with Piji Li and Wei Bi.

My primary research interests lie in the area of natural language processing and machine learning. My research aims to build robust and explainable AI systems that can understand natural language text and solve real-world problems, concerning both performance and social implications. Toward this goal, my work spans LLM alignment, long-range planning, and applications in mathematical reasoning.

Retrieved Sequence Augmentation for Protein Representation Learning

We introduce Retrieved Sequence Augmentation(RSA) for protein representation learning without additional alignment or pre-processing. GSM-Plus: A Comprehensive Benchmark for Evaluating the Robustness of LLMs as Mathematical Problem Solvers

We introduce the adversarial grade school math (GSM-Plus) dataset, an extension of GSM8K augmented with various mathematical perturbations. Our experiments on 25 LLMs and 4 prompting techniques show that while LLMs exhibit different levels of math reasoning abilities, their performances are far from robust. (ACL 2024)
